Attorney FAQ

What Do Volunteers Do?

Some volunteers research the background and location of the abuser, while other volunteers use that information to actually go out and perform service on Orders of Protection.

How Do I Get a Volunteer to Help with a Case?

First, you submit the following:

  • Case information necessary for the volunteer to complete the service affidavit
  • Additional information that can help volunteers locate and serve the abuser

Once you submit this information, a pool of volunteers are immediately notified. When a volunteer responds, you then ask the court to appoint that volunteer as a special process server.

You then mail the following to the appointed volunteer.

  • An original of the Summons
  • Two photocopies of the Summons
  • One copy of the Complaint

The volunteer then seeks out and performs service for the Order of Protection, attaches a copy of the Summons to an Affidavit of Service, and sends the documents back to you.

Is This Legal?

Yes. Per 735 ILCS 5/2-202, a court can appoint anyone over the age of 18 who is not party to the action as a special process server. Note that this appointment must take place prior to service.

Does the Sheriff Continue to Attempt Service after the Court Appoints a Special Process Server?

Yes. Even when the court appoints a special process server to a case, the Sheriff will continue efforts to serve process on Orders of Protection.
